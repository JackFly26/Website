Start

Samsung was founded in 1938 as a trading company. It was based in Taegu, Korea. Samsung was originally a grocery store. Its founder was Lee Byung-chull. It expanded to Seoul but left when the Korean War broke out. Lee branched out and made a sugar refinery, a textile industry, and a woolen mill. When this proved sucessful, Samsung quickly started diversifying.

Venture into Electronics

Samsung started into the electronics industry in the 1960's, starting many divisions. The initial facilities were in Suwon, South Korea. It started producing black and white TV's in 1970. In the 70's, Samsung began selling washing machines. In 1977, they started making color televisions. In 1980, Samsung started producing AC units. Samsung first went overseas in Portugal in 1982. In 1984, Samsung exported its first VCRs to the US. Its sales also exceeded 1 million won (927.59 USD).

Making Computers

Samsung started doing telecommunications in 1980. In 1983, Samsung began manufacturing personal computers. In 1991, Samsung developed its first mobile handset. In 1992, it developed a mobile phone system, the worlds first 64M DRAM, and over 10 million industrial robots. Samsung also began manufacturing in China. In 1993, Samsung made the first DVD-R.

Changing the Tech World

In 1997, Samsung became a worldwide Olympic partner. In 1998, they developed a flat-screen TV. In 1999, Samsung created a wireless internet phone and launched the first MP3 mobile phone. In 2001, they made the World Cyber Games. In 2002, they signed a patent sharing a liscense agreement with IBM. In 2003, Samsung sold over 10 million models of the SGH-T100 mobile phone. In 2005, they became the official sponsor of Chelsea Soccer. Samsung launched their Galaxy series in 2010. The first mobile phone with a curved edge screen, the Galaxy Note Edge, was released in 2014. It was also ranked as the 7th most valuable brand in the world. Samsung Pay was launched in 2015.
